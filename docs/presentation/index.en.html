Digital medication notebook

Your medicine,
calm and clear.

Piloo tidies up the home medicine cabinet: scan your boxes, track stock and expiry dates, see the day's doses, and share the notebook with a relative or a healthcare professional. A better notebook — not a gadget.

Mobile iOS & Android Web Offline-first Official BDPM database Self-hosted · GDPR
The problem

A drawer full of boxes, zero visibility.

Medicines expired without anyone noticing, no overview of stock, doses that get forgotten, and a parent's treatment tracked on a scrap of paper or a makeshift spreadsheet. Piloo replaces all of that with a clear, shared, offline notebook.

Also on desktop

The same notebook, on a bigger screen.

Alongside the mobile app, Piloo has a web version. It runs on the same data as the app — same cabinets, same inventory, same prescriptions, same sharing — and presents it on an interface built for the computer: more room, keyboard entry, a comfortable overview to manage your notebook from a desk.

Under the hood

The stack

  • Mobile — Flutter (iOS + Android), in-house offline sync
  • Web + API — Next.js 15, TypeScript
  • Data — PostgreSQL + Drizzle; shared OpenAPI contract
  • Medication database — official BDPM, updated 2×/day
  • Hosting — self-hosted (cookie-server), Cloudflare tunnel
  • Time zones — handled per cabinet (recently shipped)
The boundaries

What Piloo is not.

Not a medical device under the MDR regulation — today, no prescription validation, no interaction alerts, no clinical recommendations.
Not a substitute for the official prescription or for your doctor's or pharmacist's advice.
Not a data mine — no third-party trackers, no reselling, no monetising of health data.
__IC_SPARK__ Vision — not built yet

What's next.

Three directions to go beyond the notebook — for individuals and for healthcare professionals alike — without ever losing the simplicity.

For individuals

Two features to make the most of what you already have at home, while staying a simple memory aid.

1 · "What do I have for…?"

You type a symptom — headache, stomach ache, constipation, diarrhoea — and Piloo looks through your boxes to see which ones match. No dosing, just: "for that, you've got the right medicine, it's this one." Plus quick visual recognition by use (an icon per family).

2 · Interaction alerts

If a prescription contains a medicine that shouldn't be combined with another one you're currently taking, tapping it shows a clear warning: careful, possible interaction with such-and-such medicine.

The foundation: a model trained on medication data (BDPM + interaction references) to power symptom-based lookup and interaction detection.

⚠︎ The regulatory step, taken on purpose

Recommending a medicine for a symptom and detecting interactions are clinical functions: they bring Piloo into the scope of a medical device (MDR regulation, CE marking) — even when framed as "a suggestion, not a prescription." It's a deliberate path that requires a framework: validation, liability, compliance. Until that framework is in place, Piloo deliberately stays a tracking notebook.

For healthcare professionals

The patient ↔ pro bridge already exists in embryo: a pro account can follow several notebooks, with the daily round and adherence per patient. The vision is to turn it into a real patient-management tool — for a pharmacist, a nurse or a professional caregiver following several people day to day.

  • All their patients at a glance — ongoing treatments, today's adherence, alerts (missed doses, low stock, expiries).
  • Invite a patient and follow their notebook remotely, with their consent — Editor / Viewer roles, revocable at any time.
  • Prioritise the day: who hasn't taken their treatment, who's running low, who has an expired box.
  • All on the desktop version, built to manage several records without friction.
